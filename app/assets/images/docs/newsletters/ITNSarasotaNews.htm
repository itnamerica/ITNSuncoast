The Lighthouse Program

Generous funding from the 
Jerome & Mildred Paddock Foundation made it possible for ITNSarasota to partner with the Lighthouse of Manasota and transport their students  to weekly classes and internships at work sites throughout Sarasota this summer.
 

"ITNSarasota believes that it is essential for those with visual challenges to have every opportunity possible to live an independent lifestyle and we were happy to be a partner in this venture." said Scott Carpenter, ITNSarasota Board Chairman.  

 

It was a wonderful experience for ITNSarasota and our drivers. Some long lasting friendships were formed. The internships were particularly exciting for the students. We were able to take them to Jungle Gardens, to the Art Center of Sarasota, Computer Advantage, even to the South Florida Museum, home of Snooty the Manatee!

 
Community Foundation Grant Award                       

 

ITNSarasota is pleased to announce that it has been awarded a grant of $50,000 from the James Franklin Warnell & Dorothy J. Warnell Fund of the Community Foundation of Sarasota County to continue to provide transportation for seniors and the visually impaired.

 

"Since our launch in 2008, the Community Foundation has supported and encouraged the mission of ITNSarasota and we are grateful for their continued support," said Scott Carpenter, ITNSarasota Board Chairman. "Through this grant that helps pay for the actual cost of delivering a ride, seniors in our community are given new-found freedom and mobility through the dignified transportation service ITN uniquely provides."

 

More than 600 times each month, a corps of volunteer drivers transport ITNSarasota members to destinations such as doctor's appointments, dental appointments, lectures, lunches, dinners and the performing arts.  One rider said recently that she wouldn't be able to leave her home without the benefit of ITNSarasota services.
